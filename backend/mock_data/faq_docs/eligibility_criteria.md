# Eligibility Criteria — Frequently Asked Questions

## Who is eligible to apply for a property-backed loan?

### Age
- **Minimum age:** 21 years at application
- **Maximum age:** 65 years at loan maturity (i.e., you must fully repay by age 65)

### Employment / Income
| Category | Minimum Monthly Income | Minimum Employment Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Salaried | ₹25,000 | 12 months at current employer |
| Self-employed professional | ₹35,000 | 24 months business vintage |
| Business owner | ₹35,000 | 24 months business vintage |
| Retired (pension) | ₹20,000 | N/A |

### Credit Score
- **Standard applications:** Minimum CIBIL/bureau score of **650**
- **Fast-track (bank-tied property):** Minimum score of **600**
- Scores below 600 are not eligible under any product

### Nationality
- Indian resident citizens
- NRIs with valid OCI/PIO card (subject to FEMA guidelines)

## What credit score do I need?

We use the median of your CIBIL, Experian, and Equifax bureau scores. The minimum is **650 for standard applications**. Scores in the 650–699 range are eligible but attract a higher interest rate and a reduced loan-to-value cap.

Here is what different score ranges typically mean for your application:

| Score Range | Rating | Likely Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 800–900 | Excellent | Best rates, maximum LTV, fast processing |
| 700–799 | Good | Standard rates, full eligibility |
| 650–699 | Fair | Higher rate (+0.75%), reduced LTV (65%), may need co-applicant |
| 600–649 | Poor | Only eligible under fast-track (tied property), much higher rate |
| Below 600 | Very Poor | Not eligible |

## Does being an existing customer of the bank help?

Yes, significantly. Existing customers with an active mortgage registered with us benefit from:
- **Fast-track processing** (3 days instead of 7–10)
- **Relaxed credit score threshold** (600 instead of 650)
- **Reduced documentation** — no fresh KYC required
- **Slightly lower interest rate** (0.25% discount)
- **Higher DTI tolerance** (60% instead of 55%)

---

## What is a Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio and how does it affect eligibility?

DTI is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward all loan EMIs combined — existing loans plus the proposed new EMI.

**Formula:** DTI = (Total monthly EMIs ÷ Gross monthly income) × 100

- **Comfortable (0–35%):** Strong eligibility, eligible for rate discount
- **Moderate (35–45%):** Standard eligibility
- **Stretched (45–55%):** Eligible but at higher rate; loan amount may be reduced
- **Over limit (above 55%):** Not eligible for standard product (60% allowed for fast-track)

**Example:** If your income is ₹1,00,000/month and you already pay ₹20,000 in EMIs, your existing DTI is 20%. If the proposed loan would add ₹25,000 EMI, your total DTI becomes 45% — within the moderate band.

## What is the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio?

LTV is the loan amount as a percentage of the property's market value. RBI mandates the following maximums:

| Property Market Value | Maximum LTV |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹30 lakh | 90% |
| ₹30 lakh to ₹75 lakh | 80% |
| Above ₹75 lakh | 75% |

Additional LTV reductions apply for:
- Property age over 20 years: −5%
- Active legal dispute on property: −10%
- Leasehold ownership: −5%
- Property tax arrears: −3%
- High crime area (score > 60): −5%
- Flood zone: −10%

---

## Can I apply if I have an existing loan?

Yes, existing loans do not disqualify you. However, all existing EMIs are counted in your DTI calculation, which reduces the amount you can additionally borrow. The system will automatically calculate the maximum new EMI you can afford without breaching the DTI limit.

---

## I was rejected earlier at another bank. Can I still apply here?

Yes. Each bank has its own assessment criteria. However, multiple recent loan enquiries (visible on your credit report) can temporarily lower your bureau score. We recommend waiting 3–6 months between applications if previously rejected.

---

## Can a property with an existing mortgage be used as collateral?

Yes, if the existing mortgage is with our bank (a "tied" property). In that case, a top-up or additional loan is processed on a fast-track basis.

If the existing mortgage is with another bank, you will need to either:
1. Get a NOC from the existing lender, or
2. Refinance the existing loan to our bank as part of the new application

Properties with mortgages at other banks cannot directly be used as collateral without resolving the existing charge first.
