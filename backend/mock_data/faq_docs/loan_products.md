# Loan Products — Frequently Asked Questions

## What loan products does the bank offer against property?

We offer three main products secured against residential or commercial property:

### 1. Home Loan (HL)
A loan for purchasing or constructing a residential property.
- **Loan amount:** ₹5 lakh to ₹5 crore
- **Tenure:** 5 to 25 years
- **Who can apply:** Salaried employees and self-employed professionals
- **Processing fee:** 0.50% of loan amount
- **Prepayment penalty:** Nil (as per RBI guidelines)

### 2. Loan Against Property (LAP)
Mortgage your existing owned property to get funds for any legitimate purpose — business expansion, medical expenses, education, or debt consolidation.
- **Loan amount:** ₹10 lakh to ₹10 crore
- **Tenure:** 1 to 15 years
- **Who can apply:** Property owners — salaried, self-employed, or business owners
- **Processing fee:** 1.00% of loan amount
- **Prepayment penalty:** 2% of outstanding amount if prepaid before 12 months

### 3. Top-Up Loan (TUL)
An additional loan on top of your existing home loan with us, at preferential rates.
- **Loan amount:** ₹2 lakh to ₹1 crore
- **Tenure:** 1 to 10 years
- **Who can apply:** Existing home loan customers of this bank only
- **Processing fee:** 0.25% of loan amount
- **Prepayment penalty:** Nil

---

## What is the difference between a Home Loan and a Loan Against Property?

A **Home Loan** is specifically for buying or building a house — the funds are disbursed directly to the seller or builder. A **Loan Against Property** lets you mortgage a property you already own and use the funds for any purpose. LAP typically carries a slightly higher interest rate than a home loan because the end-use is unrestricted.

---

## Can I apply if I am self-employed?

Yes. Self-employed professionals (doctors, architects, CAs) and business owners are eligible. You will need to provide 2 years of ITR, audited financial statements, bank statements for 12 months, and GST returns if applicable. The minimum loan tenure for self-employed applicants is 12 months.

---

## What is the maximum loan amount I can get?

The maximum loan depends on three factors:
1. **Your income** — typically up to 60× your annual income for salaried applicants and 48× for self-employed
2. **Property value** — the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio caps the loan at 75–90% of the property's market value depending on the loan amount
3. **Your repayment capacity** — total EMIs (existing + proposed) must not exceed 50% of your gross monthly income

The bank's system automatically calculates the best eligible amount based on all three constraints.

## How long does loan approval take?

- **Standard applications:** 7–10 working days after all documents are submitted
- **Fast-track (existing customers with bank-mortgaged property):** 3 working days
- **Conditional approvals:** Timeline depends on fulfilment of conditions

---

## Is there a cooling-off period after sanction?

Yes. You have **3 working days** after receiving the sanction letter to cancel the loan without any penalty, as per RBI Fair Practices guidelines.

---

## What is the processing fee and when is it charged?

The processing fee is charged at the time of loan disbursement, not at application stage. It covers legal verification, property valuation, and credit assessment costs. The fee is non-refundable once the loan is disbursed.

---

## Can two people apply together for a loan?

Yes, joint applications are accepted. Both applicants' incomes are combined for eligibility calculation, and both are equally liable for repayment. Common joint applicants are spouses, parents and children, or business partners.
